Mulberry Tree

A compact guide: scientific names, fun facts, cultural stories, and benefits.

Names
Common & scientific identification

Common name

Mulberry Tree

Scientific name

Morus spp.
Notable species: Morus alba (White mulberry), Morus nigra (Black mulberry), Morus rubra (Red mulberry).
Fun facts
Quick and surprising
  • Silk connection: Leaves of Morus alba are the primary food of the silkworm (Bombyx mori), hence essential for silk production.
  • Fast growth: Mulberries can grow quickly in favorable conditions — young trees can shoot up remarkably fast in summer.
  • Wildlife magnet: Their sweet ripe berries attract many birds and small mammals.
  • Natural dye: The dark juice of mulberries has historically been used as a natural pigment.
  • Edible fruit: Mulberries are eaten fresh, dried, or used in jams and drinks.
Cultural stories
Legend, folklore & traditions

Pyramus & Thisbe — Greek myth

A tragic tale in which two lovers agreed to meet beneath a white mulberry tree. After a tragic misunderstanding they died and their blood-stained the berries — a mythical explanation for the red/purple color of some mulberries.

In India

Called Shahtoot in some Indian languages, mulberries appear in Ayurvedic texts and regional folklore. The tree is sometimes considered auspicious and linked with home prosperity.

China & silk culture

Because of its intimate link to sericulture, the white mulberry is culturally important in China; it is associated with silk production, agriculture and feminine craft traditions.

Health & Environmental benefits
Why mulberry matters

Health benefits

Nutritious: Vitamins C & K, iron, calcium, and antioxidants.
Blood sugar support: Compounds that may help regulate glucose.
Digestion: Good dietary fiber content aids regularity.
Heart health: May help reduce cholesterol and support cardiovascular health.

Environmental benefits

Erosion control: Strong roots stabilize soil.
Urban greening: Provides shade, oxygen, and cooling.
Wildlife support: Berries feed birds and pollinators.
Low maintenance: Hardy and drought-tolerant once established.
